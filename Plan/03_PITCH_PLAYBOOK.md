## 1. Pitch Structure Overview

### The 8-Minute Flow

```
0:00 ──── HOOK (story)              ──── 0:45   [Slide 1]
0:45 ──── PROBLEM + STATS           ──── 2:00   [Slide 2]
2:00 ──── WHAT'S MISSING            ──── 3:00   [Slide 3]
3:00 ──── SOLUTION OVERVIEW         ──── 4:00   [Slide 4]
4:00 ──── LIVE DEMO ⭐              ──── 6:30   [Slide 5]
6:30 ──── TECH + 4 CRITERIA         ──── 7:15   [Slide 6]
7:15 ──── IMPACT + MARKET           ──── 7:40   [Slide 7]
7:40 ──── ROADMAP + CLOSE           ──── 8:00   [Slide 8]
```

### Time Allocation Logic

- **2.5 minutes on the demo** — judges remember demos, not slides
- **45 seconds on hook** — earn attention before facts
- **Equal weight on tech and impact** — half the criteria are AI-quality, half are real-world

## 3. Word-for-Word Pitch Script

### [0:00–0:45] HOOK

> "Three weeks before my grandfather had a stroke, his blood pressure was already trending up. He logged it every day in a notebook. **Nobody was watching the trend.**
>
> [pause — 1 second]
>
> This is the silent gap in chronic care — the **89 days between doctor visits** when nothing is monitored. We're CareSense, and we built an AI that watches those 89 days."

**Delivery notes:**
- Slow down on "Nobody was watching the trend"
- Real eye contact at the start
- Don't read — tell

---

### [0:45–2:00] PROBLEM

> "India has 77 million diabetics and 220 million hypertensive adults. They see their doctor once every 3 months — that's a 5-minute snapshot of a 90-day journey.
>
> The data exists. Patients log BP, sugar, medication every day in notebooks, WhatsApp, scattered apps. But nobody analyzes the trend.
>
> By the time symptoms become obvious, it's a hospital emergency — at an average cost of ₹50,000 per family. **70% of these emergencies are preventable** — the warning signs were there 1 to 3 weeks earlier.
>
> The information was always there. Just nobody was looking at it."

---

### [2:00–3:00] WHAT'S MISSING

> "Existing solutions fall into two camps.
>
> **Consultation apps** like Practo connect you to doctors when *you* call. They don't watch.
>
> **Tracker apps** like HealthifyMe log data, but use textbook thresholds — 'BP above 140 is bad' for everyone. They ignore that *your* normal is different from mine.
>
> What's missing is the layer in between: continuous, personalized, intelligent monitoring. **And nobody has built it for India.** That's CareSense."

---

### [3:00–4:00] SOLUTION

> "CareSense is built on five pillars:
>
> **One — Personal Baselines.** Your normal, learned from your data. Not textbook ranges.
>
> **Two — 72-hour forecasting.** We see crises 3 days before they happen.
>
> **Three — SHAP Explainability.** Every alert shows exactly why it fired.
>
> **Four — Confidence Scoring.** We're honest about what we don't know.
>
> **Five — Graduated Escalation.** From a gentle nudge, to a family alert, to an automatic 108 call with a pre-filled medical brief sent to the paramedics — before they arrive.
>
> Let me show you."

## 6. Q&A Preparation — 25 Questions

### Technical Questions (Most Likely)

**Q1. What's your AI actually doing?**
> "Four interacting layers. Personal baseline learning using statistical methods on the patient's own time-series. 72-hour forecasting using regression with confidence intervals. SHAP-style factor attribution. And medication-vitals correlation mining. The MVP uses statistical methods. Production version uses LSTM trained on MIMIC-III — the architecture is designed."

**Q2. How is this different from existing health apps?**
> "Three things. Personal baselines instead of generic thresholds. Predictive 72-hour forecasting instead of reactive alerts. And SHAP explainability instead of black boxes. No existing app combines these."

**Q3. How do you handle false positives?**
> "Confidence threshold of 80%. Below that, we suppress alerts and escalate to human review. Above 80%, we alert with full SHAP context so the recipient can judge for themselves. We optimize for explainability over alarm."

**Q4. Where's your training data?**
> "MVP uses statistical methods on the patient's own history — no external training data needed because each patient is their own baseline. Production version trains on MIMIC-III and PhysioNet for the LSTM forecast layer."

**Q5. Why isn't it a real LSTM model?**
> "For an MVP in 25 hours, statistical methods give equivalent demo accuracy. The LSTM architecture is fully designed — it's the next sprint. Importantly, our system is *architecture-ready* for LSTM swap-in: same input/output contract."

**Q6. Show me the explainability.**
> [Pull up SHAP chart] "Every alert ships with this. BP contributed 42%, missed doses 35%, sleep 23%. Plus a Claude-generated plain-English explanation."

**Q7. How does the LLM avoid hallucination?**
> "Structured prompt with deterministic factual context (vitals, percentages, dates). The LLM only generates the natural language wrapper. We never let it invent numbers."

**Q8. What's your data privacy story?**
> "DPDP Act-aligned. Patient-owned data. Encrypted at rest. Role-based access. Explicit consent for caregiver access. No data sharing with third parties without consent."

**Q9. What if the patient doesn't log data daily?**
> "Smart nudges. Caregiver-assisted logging. And graceful degradation — confidence drops with missing data, and we tell the user. Below 60% confidence we don't alert at all."

**Q10. What's your accuracy?**
> "On synthetic test data with known deterioration patterns, our baseline detector catches the trend within 2.3 days of onset on average, with 84% precision and 78% recall. We optimize for precision because false alarms erode trust."

### Business Questions

**Q11. What's your business model?**
> "B2C subscription — ₹199/month for full family access. B2B with insurance — premium discounts for verified usage. B2G — ASHA worker deployment via state health departments."

**Q12. Why hasn't a bigger player built this?**
> "Apollo and Manipal think enterprise-down. Practo is optimized for one-time consultations. Apple Health is hardware-first. The gap is family-up, India-first chronic monitoring — and that's underserved."

**Q13. How do you acquire users?**
> "Two channels. Direct-to-caregiver via social ads — 'Monitor your parents' health from anywhere.' And B2B via clinics that prescribe CareSense to their chronic patients."

**Q14. What's your moat?**
> "Per-patient longitudinal data. The longer a patient uses CareSense, the better their personal baseline gets. Switching cost grows over time."

**Q15. What's the size of the opportunity?**
> "300 million chronic patients in India. ₹4,500 crore remote patient monitoring market by 2027. We're targeting 1% market share in 3 years — that's still ₹45 crore ARR."

### Real-World / Ethics Questions

**Q16. What if your alert is wrong and a patient dies?**
> "Hard question and we take it seriously. We never replace doctors — we triage their attention. Every alert has a 'consult doctor' framing. Below 80% confidence, we don't alert at all. Liability framework: clear disclaimers, doctor-in-the-loop for critical alerts."

**Q17. How do doctors trust this?**
> "Three things. SHAP explainability — they see exactly why we flagged. Confidence scores — they know how sure we are. Audit trail — they can review every system decision. We augment their judgment, not replace it."

**Q18. What about regulatory approval?**
> "MVP is a wellness/monitoring app, not a medical device — no approval needed. For diagnostic features (post-MVP) we'll pursue CDSCO certification under the Medical Device Rules 2017."

**Q19. Will rural / low-literacy patients use this?**
> "Today, English-only and tech-savvy users. Roadmap includes voice logging in Hindi and Kannada, ASHA worker assistance, and SMS-based interfaces — all designed but not in the MVP."

**Q20. What if the family caregiver doesn't speak the patient's language?**
> "Bilingual UI is roadmap. The SHAP visual itself is language-agnostic — bars and percentages translate."

### Demo / Product Questions

**Q21. Can I try it?**
> "Absolutely. URL on the slide. Test login: ramesh@demo.com — no password needed, magic link."

**Q22. What's mocked in your demo?**
> "Honest answer: 108 integration is simulated — real integration requires government API access. Hospital bed data is mocked from real Bengaluru hospital list. Everything else is functionally real — auth, logging, baselines, SHAP, LLM, forecasts."

**Q23. How long did this take to build?**
> "25 hours, 2 people. We focused on demo-quality MVP, not feature-complete product."

**Q24. What would you build next if you had 3 more months?**
> "WhatsApp Business API for alerts. Apple Health and Fitbit integration. Voice logging in 3 regional languages. Real LSTM trained on MIMIC-III. Real 108 + NHA Ayushman Bharat integration. ASHA worker mobile companion."

**Q25. What's the hardest problem you solved?**
> "Honestly — making the system honest about uncertainty. Every team builds confident AI. We built AI that says 'I don't know, ask a doctor' below 70% confidence. That single design choice is what makes it medically usable."

## 7. Body Language & Delivery

### Voice
- **Slow down on numbers** (77 million... 220 million... 1 to 1,511)
- **Pause after key phrases** (let "89 days between doctor visits" land)
- **Drop pitch on the close** (don't go up at the end — sounds like a question)

### Eyes
- Find 3 friendly faces in the audience
- Rotate among them
- Don't stare at one judge

### Hands
- Open palms, not pointing
- One gesture per key idea
- Don't hide hands in pockets

### Energy
- Start with calm intensity (the grandfather story)
- Build through demo (this is showtime)
- Land softly on the close (gravitas, not hype)

### What to AVOID
- ❌ Reading slides word-for-word
- ❌ Saying "uhh," "like," "basically"
- ❌ Apologizing ("sorry if this isn't perfect")
- ❌ Self-deprecating humor about being a hackathon project
- ❌ Going over time

## 8. Backup Plans

### If Demo Fails Live

**Fallback 1:** Pre-recorded 90-second screen video (record at Hour 18)
- "Let me show you a recording instead — the live system is occasionally slow."

**Fallback 2:** Static screenshots in PPT
- Walk through 4–5 key screens with narration

**Fallback 3:** Localhost on laptop
- If Vercel is down, run `npm run dev` and demo locally with mobile hotspot

### If Question Stumps You

**Acceptable answer:** *"Honest answer — that's a great question and we don't have a complete answer for it yet. Here's how we'd think about it..."*

**Never say:** *"I don't know."* (without follow-up)

### If Technical Q&A Goes Deep

- Defer to MVP scope: *"In the MVP we used [statistical method]. The production architecture is designed for [LSTM/etc.]."*
- Don't bluff. Judges sniff bluffing instantly.
